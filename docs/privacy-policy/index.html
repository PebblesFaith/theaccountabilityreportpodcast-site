Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy details

This Privacy Policy explains how The Accountability Report Podcast handles website information, contact information, story tip descriptions, documentation descriptions, and user communications. Until public submission features are formally activated, visitors should not submit sensitive personal information through public forms.

A finalized Privacy Policy should be reviewed before any story tip submission, contact form, document upload, newsletter, analytics package, or database-backed intake feature becomes active.

1. Current Website Status

The Accountability Report Podcast website is currently being developed as an informational public-interest media website. Some forms, contact features, story tip features, and submission tools may appear as design placeholders before they are connected to a live review process.

Visitors should treat inactive forms as non-operational. The website should not be used to submit passwords, Social Security numbers, full account numbers, medical records, financial credentials, private legal documents, security codes, or other sensitive personal information.

2. Information We May Collect

Once active, the website may collect information that a visitor voluntarily provides, such as a name, email address, topic category, date of incident, concern summary, supporting documentation description, correction request, general inquiry, or other communication submitted through an approved website process.

The website may also process limited technical information needed to operate, secure, troubleshoot, or improve the website, such as browser type, device type, general request information, date and time of access, referral information, and server log information.

3. Story Tips and Documentation Descriptions

Story tip submission features are not active until privacy, storage, review, retention, and safety procedures are finalized. When activated, story tip submissions may be reviewed for editorial relevance, documentation value, public-interest value, and possible future reporting.

Submitting a story tip does not guarantee publication, investigation, response, legal advice, agency action, or confidential treatment. Visitors should not submit unnecessary private identifiers or sensitive records through any public-facing form.

4. How Information May Be Used

Information submitted through approved website channels may be used to review public-interest concerns, respond to inquiries, evaluate correction requests, organize documentation, improve website content, prepare podcast topics, assess editorial relevance, and maintain website security.

The podcast may summarize or discuss submitted concerns only when appropriate editorial review has been completed and when the presentation is consistent with privacy, safety, documentation, and public-interest standards.

5. Information Sharing

The Accountability Report Podcast does not intend to sell personal information. Information may be shared only when needed for website operation, legal compliance, safety review, editorial review, professional consultation, service-provider support, or when the visitor gives permission.

If a matter appears to involve immediate danger, unlawful conduct, or a legal obligation, information may be reviewed or handled according to the circumstances and applicable procedures.

6. Sensitive Information

Visitors should not submit sensitive personal information unless a secure process is later activated and clearly explained. Sensitive information may include passwords, Social Security numbers, full account numbers, security codes, private medical information, private financial records, private legal documents, or unrelated personal information.

If sensitive information is received unintentionally, it may be minimized, redacted, excluded from editorial use, or deleted where appropriate.

7. Data Storage and Retention

Before any active intake process is launched, the website should define how submitted information is stored, who may review it, how long it may be retained, how status changes are tracked, and how unnecessary information may be reduced or removed.

Future storage may include a controlled database for public tip submissions, review status history, and editorial notes. No live database-backed story tip storage should be activated until privacy and review procedures are finalized.

8. Security

Reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards should be used to protect website operations and submitted information. No website, email system, database, or online submission process can guarantee absolute security.

Visitors should avoid submitting sensitive information through ordinary web forms or email unless a secure process is later announced and clearly explained.

9. External Links and Platforms

The website may reference third-party websites, podcast platforms, public resources, agencies, social media platforms, or external documents. Third-party websites and services are controlled by their own privacy practices and terms.

The Accountability Report Podcast is not responsible for the privacy practices, content, or security of external websites that are not controlled by the podcast.

10. Contact and Updates

Domain-based contact addresses are planned for official public communication once active. Privacy-related questions, correction requests, and formal communications should use official domain-based addresses after they are published.

This Privacy Policy may be updated as the website, podcast, contact process, story tip review procedure, database structure, and publication workflow continue to develop.

Until the final privacy process is complete, public forms should remain inactive and should not collect sensitive personal information.
